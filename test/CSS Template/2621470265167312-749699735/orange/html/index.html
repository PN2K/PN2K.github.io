About Us

The Phyo Nyi Nyi Kyaw Memorial Foundation was founded on August 08, 2012 in Boston, Massachusetts, USA to honor the memory of Phyo N. Kyaw, an MIT Alum and college classmate who first came to MIT as an international student from Burma. The PN2K Foundation is a charity (non-profit) organisation set up in alignment with some of the issues that Phyo most cared about, like empowering high-achieving, high-potential, but underprivileged children by providing them with better access to educational resources and mentorship. The Foundation is entirely run by volunteers so that 100% of funds received go towards our development and philanthropic initiatives. You can find more information about these projects under the activities section of our website.

Band Together! (wristbands)

We now have wristbands that serve a dual purpose: (1) promote bicycle safety, especially when cycling during evenings; and (2) remind us to live life fully! The wristbands glow in the dark to keep our wearers visible in the dark and to remind us to stay safe while on the streets. The wristbands are also debossed with cheerful words that exemplify how Phyo chose to live each moment of his life.

If you would like to purchase a wristband, please Contact Us. We will be setting up an Amazon sales page soon to enable users to make online purchases as well.

All funds raised will go towards the nonprofit activities of our Foundation. In particular, these funds will be used for the scholarships and educational initiatives for our local Boston students and children in Asia we are working to help.

Bike Safety at MIT

Phyo was an avid cyclist and was active in cycling groups around Boston. He stressed safety in bicycling, so it came as a shock to all when we heard the news of Phyo’s fatal bike accident. Wandering along Massachusetts Avenue and Vassar Street, you might have seen the ghost bike that one of Phyo’s kind co-workers set up in his memory. Thank you to those who have taken the time and care to replace the flowers with fresh ones from time to time. We send lots of gratitude as well to MIT for allowing us to keep this bike there in memory of Phyo. Special thanks to Mr. N.M. from MIT facilities for taking care of this bike and locking it up inside during harsh Boston winter storms!

We are currently in the process of implementing a new bike safety program for incoming freshmen that will be held during orientation and throughout the academic year. These sessions will be made possible by our partnerships with:

  • Cambridge City Council
  • MIT DAPER (Department of Athletics, Physical Education & Recreation)
  • MIT Division for Student Life
  • MIT Undergraduate Advising and Academic Programming (UAAP)
  • MIT Campus Police
  • MIT Hubway

By retelling Phyo’s story and making more students aware of bicycling in Cambridge/Boston, we can look after each others’ safety!

Take Action!

Do you want to make cycling safer in Boston? Are you an EMT? Are you part of your school’s cycling team and want to promote your rights on the street? We would love to hear from you, especially if you are interested in helping us partner with organizations around Cambridge and Boston and organize events that promote bike safety. We are dedicated to educating incoming students about their rights and responsibilities as cyclists and about tricky intersections and dangerous streets (including familiarizing them with the streets of Cambridge and Boston via bike tours).

Have other links to share or ways in which you would like to help make cycling safer in Cambridge and Boston? Please shoot us an email at pnnkfoundation@gmail.com or visit our Contact Us page to fill out the form and we will get back to you.

Empowering Youth through Amphibious Achievement

The PN2K Memorial Foundation is partnering with MIT Amphibious Achievement (AA), a dual athletic and academic mentorship program for urban youth in Boston-Cambridge where students (called Achievers) better themselves through aquatic sports and college preparatory tutoring sessions at MIT. Amphibious Achievement is best known for its annual Erg-a-Thon, a fundraising event during which program participants show off their erg-ing skills (location for 2013 TBD).

PN2K and MIT Amphibious Achievement are collaborating on three main initiatives:

Project 1: Amphibious Achievement Erg-a-Thon

Of funds raised during the Erg-a-Thon, $500 will go towards The Phyo Nyi Nyi Kyaw Achievement Scholarship, a joint scholarship award made possible by the efforts of MIT AA and PN2K, set up in memory of Phyo and awarded to an Achiever each year. The awardee will be chosen based on their demonstration of dedication, potential, and character during his/her four years in the AA program as well as his/her participation in creative writing activities outlined by AA and PN2K. This Achievement Award will go towards the Achiever’s college expenses.



Project 2: Mentorship Support

PN2K will be working to develop a career-advising program for Achievers that will last during and beyond the four years they spend with MIT AA. This mentorship program will be open to Achievers who apply for the Phyo Nyi Nyi Kyaw Achievement Scholarship and meet the writing eligibility criteria (related to the blog project below). The advisor program aspires to leverage existing networks of young professionals to create a supportive community that will help open doors of opportunities for Achievers, both in the form of college application support and internship opportunities.

Want to be a career advisor or want to nominate someone? Contact us at pnnk-director@mit.edu or visit our Contact Us page.



Project 3: Achievers’ blogs

Achievers will be engaged in fun, inspirational, comical, creative writing exercises through the PN2K platform. AA and PN2K mentors will provide the support that Achievers need, including creative prompts and writing tips, to strengthen their critical writing, a powerful skill to learn no matter what field. The PN2K Achiever blog hopes to help Achievers develop their voices and give them a storytelling platform to exchange and share written stories with a wider, international audience. Blogs of the recipients of the Phyo Nyi Nyi Kyaw Achievement Scholarship will be featured on our website. Beyond their time with AA and PN2K, we hope that these recipients will continue to maintain their blogs on overcoming challenges and will inspire others with their powerful stories.

Check out the Amphibious Achievement website for more information and updates: http://amphibious.mit.edu/

Giving Back to Asia

Phyo cared deeply about (1) children, (2) empowerment through education, and (3) making the most of life by having new experiences. One of his aspirations had been to return to Asia to make education reforms that would empower more children with the gift of knowledge. PN2K hopes to carry through his ambitions in Asia.

Please check back later for more details! This site is a work in progress.

Kyaw Family Memorial Album

In Phyo's memory, a memorial album – containing photo collages, poems, stories, and other creative pieces by Phyo’s friends – was created and sent to the Kyaw family who lives in Burma. We thank everyone who has participated thus far for your thoughtfulness and kind efforts!

This album is an ongoing project and will continue until the album fills up. There are currently a limited number of spots left. If you want to be part of this album, please email us and we will be glad to send you further information. When you complete your album page, you will have the option to have your page displayed here on the website as well!

Projects in the Making

Recitals and Concerts

Phyo loved music, especially musicals and silly songs with a catchy tune. Whether in the hallway during study breaks or on top of a mountain during a weekend hike, he was never shy about sharing his cheerful, bold, and melodic voice with the group. With this spirit, PN2K has been reaching out to both individual performers and local Burmese groups to explore the possibility of future musical escapades. Please stay tuned for more details!






Tanuki Gifts

Gifts to spread joy and cheer, all with a tanuki (raccoon dog) theme. Why tanuki? It started out as a running joke during Phyo’s freshman year that the tanuki was his brethren animal spirit. Indeed, it became his unofficial animal mascot by the time he graduated from MIT. Designs are currently under development.
