Our Mission


PN2K's overall focus is on education. We work on education in two areas:

  1. Education in Bike Safety: PN2K works to make cycling safer for everyone, starting at MIT. MIT PN2K (our MIT-funded and ASA-approved student group at MIT) exists to work primarily on bike safety for the MIT community.
  2. Education in Academia/Scholarship: Currently our largest partner is MIT Amphibious Achievement. Our work in this area involves offering more opportunities to equal access education for underprivileged youth.

Background and Mission

BACKGROUND: The Phyo Nyi Nyi Kyaw (PN2K) Memorial Foundation was founded on August 08, 2012 and incorporated in Boston, Massachusetts, USA to honor the memory of Phyo N. Kyaw, an MIT Alum and college classmate who first came to MIT as an international student from Burma. PN2K is set up to align with some of the areas that Phyo most cared about, like empowering children through education and mentorship. Phyo was an avid cyclist and was active in cycling groups around Boston. He stressed safety in bicycling, so it came as a shock to all when we heard the news of Phyo’s fatal bike accident.

MISSION: The PN2K Memorial Foundation exists to work with organisations (majority nonprofits) that align with PN2K’s mission statement. We aim to add value to the activities of these organisations by leveraging our MIT resources, international connections, access to funding opportunities, and creativity to enhance the value that our partner organisations can bring to the community and students they help. We become very actively involved with our partners and are always looking for the chance to enhance the value of their programs. In the end, everyone wins – And we all have tons of fun making a difference!

If you would like to be considered for a potential partnership, you can read more here, but FIRST, please make sure that your organization aligns with our mission statement, which you can find in our Articles of Incorporation (please see the links below).

WHAT IS MIT AMPHIBIOUS ACHIEVEMENT? MIT Amphibious Achievement (Amphibz) is a dual athletic and academic mentorship program for urban youth in Boston-Cambridge where students (called Achievers) better themselves through aquatic sports and college preparatory tutoring sessions at MIT. The PN2K Memorial Foundation is partnering with MIT Amphibious Achievement to run three main initiatives (please see below). For the latest updates on MIT Amphibz, please visit their website: http://amphibious.mit.edu/


PROJECT 1: MIT Amphibz ERG-A-THON, THE PHYO NYI NYI KYAW ACHIEVEMENT AWARD ($500 AWARD!)

Erg-A-Thon: This annual erg-a-thon is a fundraising event during which people can jump on ergs to show off their erg-ing skills. This year, the Erg-A-Thon will be held on Thursday, April 17, 2014 on the Student Center steps. Don’t miss out!

$500 PN2K Achievement Award: Of funds raised during the Erg-a-Thon, $500 will go towards The Phyo Nyi Nyi Kyaw Achievement Award, a joint scholarship award made possible in 2013-4 by the efforts of MIT Amphibz and PN2K. This Award is set up in memory of Phyo and will be presented to an Achiever each year to continue encouraging him/her to pursue college and cover educational-related expenses. Awardees will be assessed along the following criteria: dedication, potential, vigor of character as demonstrated through scholarship and athletics, commitment. PROJECT 2: CAREER ADVISORSHIP PROGRAM

PN2K organizes exciting on-site visits to local companies, start-ups, and nonprofits for Achievers each semester to inspire them to pursue higher education and introduce them to potential career paths. Southeast Asia Initiatives

Phyo cared deeply about (1) children, (2) empowerment through education, and (3) making the most of life by having new experiences. One of his aspirations had been to return to Asia to make education reforms that would empower more children with the gift of knowledge. PN2K hopes to carry through his ambitions in Asia.

In the next 12 months, we aim to establish scholarships for international students from or within Southeast Asia who are pursuing higher education (university level) through fundraising initiatives and sponsorship support.

Fall Bike Safety Program

Q: Is this program for me?
A: Are you new to the roads of MIT and Cambridge? Are you an incoming freshman to the MIT campus? Have you been here for awhile but never ridden on a bike because you were not sure of the “Rules of the Road” or afraid of the terrifying-looking traffic? If yes, then this program is perfect for you.

This program will consist of two parts: Part 1 is an educational and fun play. Part 2 is a “hands-on” bike tour around MIT/Cambridge.

  • Part 1. Bike Safety for Dummies 101 - Participants of our bike safety program will attend a fun and informative student-led play on bike safety. They will learn about the real dangers of cycling, rules of the road, and resources for cyclists at MIT.
  • Part 2. Bike Tour around MIT/Cambridge - Participants will jump on Hubway bikes and be guided by MIT Police around the streets of MIT and Cambridge.

Spring Bike Safety Program

Q: Is this program for me?
A: Do you have a bicycle already but still are not clear about the “rules of the road”? Did you partake in the Fall Bike Safety program previously and now own a bicycle? Did your bike come from the MIT Police bike auction and might now be falling apart? Would you like a helmet and bike lights to go with your registered bike? If yes, then this program is perfect for you!

  • Part 1. Bike Safety for Dummies 202 - Participants of our bike safety program will attend a fun and informative student-led play on bike safety. They will learn about the real dangers of cycling, rules of the road, and resources for cyclists at MIT.
    • Participants will need to bring their own working bicycles and have them registered with MIT PTO. After completing Part I, participants will receive basic bike accessories for a safe ride (e.g., lights, helmets…), have their bike looked over by Cambridge Bicycle for a basic tune-up.
  • Part 2. Bike Tour around MIT/Cambridge - Participants will jump on their own bikes (or Hubway bikes if their bikes are not safe for riding) and be guided by MIT Police around the streets of MIT and Cambridge.

MIT PN2K OFFICERS


All officers of this organization must be distinct people and current MIT students:
  • President/Co-Presidents
    The president/co-presidents will coordinate with the directors of PN2K, Inc., the President(s) is in charge of making sure that MIT PN2K activities align with the missions of the PN2K foundation. The president should work closely with the directors of each team to understand and oversee operations and make decisions when conflicting viewpoints arise.
  • Treasurer
    The treasurer shall be responsible for the finances of the group, including preparing financial reports during the year as asked by the directors of PN2K. The treasurer is required to sign all checks/vouchers of the group and keep an exact account of all receipts and expenditures and report them to the PN2K Corporation Treasurer and PN2K Corporation Co-Directors.
  • Bike Ambassador Coordinator
    The bike safety ambassador coordinator will be responsible for planning, organizing, and implementing the two annual bike safety programs, once during the fall semester (orientation) and another during the spring semester. They will be responsible for coordinating all communications with MIT departments, programs, and other MIT organizations.
  • Educational Programs Ambassador Coordinator
    The educational programs ambassador coordinator will be responsible for coordinating, organizing, and implementing work with existing partners of PN2K (e.g., including MIT student group Amphibious Achievement, as outlined in the PN2K-MIT AA Terms and Conditions agreement) to promote education, literacy, philanthropy and sportsmanship.
  • Marketing Officer
    Manage and work with the marketing committee to test out creative ways to market the PN2K brand, develop product lines, and promote PN2K visibility (including the use of social media outlets and newsletters, videos, or other materials).
  • Webmaster
    Manages the IT infrastructure, including development of MIT PN2K website and related IT infrastructure.
  • Human Resource Officer
    Recruit new members into MIT PN2K; once the teams are formed, HR ensures positive team dynamics through social events and group bonding activities (e.g., annual retreats).
  • Finance/Logistics Officer
    This person will be responsible for the logistics of products ordered for MIT PN2K, including managing the flow of goods; they will help to book room for events, review our budget, help to prepare when MIT PN2K applies for group funding, maintain receipts, collect money from fundraising events, etc.
  • Fundraising Officer
    Manages the fundraising team and works with local community partners to organize fundraising events towards our bike safety and youth scholarship initiatives.

About MIT PN2K

The group (MIT PN2K) is the MIT chapter of the Phyo Nyi Nyi Kyaw (PN2K) Memorial Foundation, a registered nonprofit in Massachusetts that has been in existence since its incorporation in August 2012. This group exists primarily to focus on bike safety for the MIT community with the goal of decreasing cycling fatalities and serious injuries by holding two large "Bike Safety @ MIT" events each year (in the Fall and Spring), as well as year-round bike safety awareness events. Our bike safety events will be interactive and educate MIT cyclists on rules of the road. Participants will either jump on Hubway bikes or register their own bikes with MIT PTO (in case bikes are stolen), receive free basic bike tune-ups and necessary bike safety equipment from MIT PN2K/PN2K (example: bike lights, helmets, etc. for free or at a heavily subsidized prices).

MIT PN2K’s other initiative is on youth scholarship. For this, we aim to increase educational opportunities for high-potential students from disadvantaged backgrounds. We will partner with MIT organizations, such as MIT Amphibious Achievement, and outside nonprofits and institutions (e.g. the Yangon Institute of Technology in Myanmar) to develop innovative (STEM) educational programs and opportunities for high-potential students who may not otherwise have such access to educational resources.
