The wristbands are also debossed with three words that exemplify how Phyo chose to live each moment of his life: Cherish. Share. Grow.

How can I join?

Donate now to join our campaign, and we will contact you about your free wristband.

Where will my donations go?

All funds raised will go towards the development of our nonprofit activities. In particular, we are aiming to raise $5K to establish academic scholarships and educational initiatives for underprivileged children in Asia who wish to pursue higher-level education.

Bike Safety at MIT

Wandering along Massachusetts Avenue and Vassar Street, you might have seen the ghost bike that one of Phyo’s kind co-workers, Andrea Schneider, set up in his memory. Thank you to those who have taken the time and care to replace the flowers with fresh ones from time to time. We send lots of gratitude as well to MIT for allowing us to keep this bike there in memory of Phyo. Special thanks to Mr. Norman Magnuson from MIT facilities for taking care of this bike and locking it up inside during harsh Boston winter storms!


WHAT: We are in the process of working with MIT Campus Police to implement a new bicycle safety program for the MIT community to be held during MIT Orientation of 2014. This program will have both an educational and “hands-on” component, where students will have the chance to put into practice the safe biking skills taught in less than an hour. We hope that by retelling Phyo’s story and informing the MIT community of the real dangers of cycling, we can look after each other’s safety!


PARTNERS: Thank you to the following supporters that we will be working with to make this bike safety event happen!

  • MIT Parking & Transportation Office: Robynn Cruz-Walker (Commuter Program Administrator)
  • MIT Police: Sergeant Cheryl Vossmer, Deputy Chief Jay Perault, Chief John DiFava
  • Cambridge City Council: Leland Cheung (City Councillor)
  • MIT Undergraduate Advising and Academic Programming (UAAP): Associate Dean Julie Norman (Senior Associate Dean for Undergraduate Education & Director)
  • Community Wellness at MIT Medical: Susanna (Zan) Barry (Senior Program Manager)

WHAT IS MIT AMPHIBIOUS ACHIEVEMENT? MIT Amphibious Achievement (AA) is a dual athletic and academic mentorship program for urban youth in Boston-Cambridge where students (called Achievers) better themselves through aquatic sports and college preparatory tutoring sessions at MIT. The PN2K Memorial Foundation is partnering with MIT Amphibious Achievement to run three main initiatives (please see below). For the latest updates on MIT AA, please visit their website: http://amphibious.mit.edu/


PROJECT 1: MIT AA ERG-A-THON, THE PHYO NYI NYI KYAW ACHIEVEMENT AWARD ($500 AWARD!)

Erg-A-Thon: This annual erg-a-thon is a fundraising event during which people can jump on ergs to show off their erg-ing skills. This year, the Erg-A-Thon will be held on Thursday, April 17, 2014 on the Student Center steps. Don’t miss out!

$500 PN2K Achievement Award: Of funds raised during the Erg-a-Thon, $500 will go towards The Phyo Nyi Nyi Kyaw Achievement Award, a joint scholarship award made possible in 2013-4 by the efforts of MIT AA and PN2K. This Award is set up in memory of Phyo and will be presented to an Achiever each year to continue encouraging him/her to pursue college and cover educational-related expenses. Awardees will be assessed along the following criteria: dedication, potential, vigor of character as demonstrated through scholarship and athletics, commitment. PROJECT 2: CAREER ADVISORSHIP PROGRAM

PN2K organizes exciting on-site visits to local companies, start-ups, and nonprofits for Achievers each semester to inspire them to pursue higher education and introduce them to potential career paths. This mentorship program will be open to all Achievers. The Career Advising program aspires to leverage existing networks of young professionals to create a supportive community that will help open doors of opportunities for Achievers.

Upcoming Tours:

  • Danger!Awesome: SATURDAY, APRIL 5th @11 AM
  • EcoVent: FRIDAY, MAY 2nd @5 PM

PROJECT 3: ACHIEVERS’ SPOTLIGHT

Near the end of each year, Achievers will be asked to reflect on their high school and Amphibious Achievement journey. Along with being a creative writing exercise, this Achievers’ Spotlight will also feature some of these Achievers’ writing pieces as stories of inspiration and hope. For instance, Achievers will be asked to reflect on their life journey towards overcoming obstacles, triumphant moments, inspiration memories, major people of influence, and what they feel grateful for in their lives.

Southeast Asia Initiatives

Phyo cared deeply about (1) children, (2) empowerment through education, and (3) making the most of life by having new experiences. One of his aspirations had been to return to Asia to make education reforms that would empower more children with the gift of knowledge. PN2K hopes to carry through his ambitions in Asia.

In the next 12 months, we aim to establish scholarships for international students from or within Southeast Asia who are pursuing higher education (university level) through fundraising initiatives and sponsorship support.

Please join us in our mission to improve the educational opportunities of these children! Donate now or Contact Us for more information about other ways to help.

Kyaw Family Memorial Album

In Phyo's memory, a memorial album – containing photo collages, poems, stories, and other creative pieces by Phyo’s friends – was created and sent to the Kyaw family who lives in Burma. We thank everyone who has participated thus far for your thoughtfulness and kind efforts! If you would like to contribute to the album in any way, shape, or form, let us know so we can reserve a page for you. Your “masterpiece” can take any form – photos, drawings, a private sealed letter taped to the page and decorated with gold leaves, etc. – it is really up to you!

This album is an ongoing project and will continue until the album fills up. There are currently a limited number of spots left. If you want to be part of this album, please email us and we will be glad to send you further information. When you complete your album page, you will have the option to have your page displayed here on the website as well!

Projects in the Making

Recitals and Concerts

Phyo loved music, especially musicals and silly songs with a catchy tune. Whether in the hallway during study breaks or on top of a mountain during a weekend hike, he was never shy about sharing his cheerful, bold, and melodic voice with the group. With this spirit, PN2K has been reaching out to both individual performers and local Burmese groups to explore the possibility of future musical escapades. Please stay tuned for more details!






Tanuki Gifts

Gifts to spread joy and cheer, all with a tanuki (raccoon dog) theme. Why tanuki? It started out as a running joke during Phyo’s freshman year that the tanuki was his brethren animal spirit. Indeed, it became his unofficial animal mascot by the time he graduated from MIT. Designs are currently under development.

Background and Mission

BACKGROUND: The Phyo Nyi Nyi Kyaw (PN2K) Memorial Foundation was founded on August 08, 2012 and incorporated in Boston, Massachusetts, USA to honor the memory of Phyo N. Kyaw, an MIT Alum and college classmate who first came to MIT as an international student from Burma. PN2K is set up to align with some of the areas that Phyo most cared about, like empowering children through education and mentorship. Phyo was an avid cyclist and was active in cycling groups around Boston. He stressed safety in bicycling, so it came as a shock to all when we heard the news of Phyo’s fatal bike accident.

MISSION: The PN2K Memorial Foundation exists to work with organisations (majority nonprofits) that align with PN2K’s mission statement. We aim to add value to the activities of these organisations by leveraging our MIT resources, international connections, access to funding opportunities, and creativity to enhance the value that our partner organisations can bring to the community and students they help. We become very actively involved with our partners and are always looking for the chance to enhance the value of their programs. In the end, everyone wins – And we all have tons of fun making a difference!

If you would like to be considered for a potential partnership, you can read more here, but FIRST, please make sure that your organization aligns with our mission statement, which you can find in our Articles of Incorporation (please see the links below).

We are recruiting for pro-active leaders and do-ers who can help shape our foundation and build up the following teams:

  • Fundraising (active money collectors) Goal of position: generate funds for project collaborations; ideate on local cafés and donors to reach out to; search for grants and write grant proposals; be active in fundraising, so preferably someone who likes promoting our cause and attending events where we can give out wristbands to supportive donors
  • Marketing/Promotion (idea generators) Goal of position: market the PN2K brand to the public and organisations/companies to increase visibility; develop promotional materials and new product lines; maintain social media updates; create newsletters, pamphlets, promotional videos, and other materials that are used when fundraising and as updates to donors
  • Outreach (partnership hunters) – currently not open Goal of position: establish new partnerships that are meaningful and in line with our mission; collect data on different non-profit organisations; increase our reach in the local community but also look out for opportunities to expand internationally
  • Community Ambassador Groups (public relations officers) Goal of position: ideate on where and how PN2K can add value to our existing partnerships; serve as point persons/liaisons for existing partnerships (e.g., MIT AA, MIT bike safety programs, Danger!Awesome, EcoVent, etc.); recruit volunteers in local communities to join our fundraising initiatives
  • Technical Group/IT Support Team Goal of position: provide IT support and maintain our website to make sure it is constantly updated with information that can be used for marketing and informational purposes.
  • Finance Group - currently not open Goal of position: review our budget on a quarterly, annual, etc. basis; be able to send financial reports to directors when asked; approach and assess our activities from a financial perspective and advise on how current and prospective activities impact the organization from a financial angle

We are always up for people who want to add value to our partnerships, join our programs, or create something new. Be a positive influence in a young student’s life. If you want to do something shorter term, look no further:

  • Work with PN2K and MIT Amphibious Achievement to become a role model and PN2K ‘career’ advisor for our high school students in the Boston- Cambridge (Massachusetts) community. Alternatively, if you are part of a start-up that can offer a 60-minute on-site tour to aspiring college students (and be featured on our webpage), get in touch!
  • Work with us to implement a Bike Safety event during MIT Orientation of 2014. We are working with high-level administration and MIT staff, so this is an exciting opportunity to bring something new and meaningful to the MIT community.
  • Do you have personal connections in Southeast Asia that you would like to see form a potential partnership with PN2K for the disbursement of educational scholarships? Do you have ideas for who we can work with (people, schools, organisations, etc.) to improve students’ quality of education? Let us know!
  • Are you a product designer or aspire to be one? Do you need to build your art portfolio and work experience? Join our fun team and let your imagination flow by creating new designs for existing (or new!) promotional products for PN2K– drop us an email at pnnk-director@mit.edu. We also love receiving sample artworks you have done in the past that demonstrate your creativity!

We love our volunteers for they make the world go round! If you have other ideas or want to help in other ways, do not hesitate to contact us
