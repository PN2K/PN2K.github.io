Additional Projects by MIT PN2K for Bike Safety

We will be working with the following partners to implement a 2-part bike safety event at MIT, which will take place twice during the year - once in the Fall and the other during the Spring.

  • MIT Police
  • MIT Parking & Transporation Office
  • Community Wellness at MIT Medical


Fall Bike Safety Program

Q: Is this program for me?
A: Are you new to the roads of MIT and Cambridge? Are you an incoming freshman to the MIT campus? Have you been here for awhile but never ridden on a bike because you were not sure of the rules of the road or afraid of the terrifying-looking traffic? If yes, then this program is perfect for you.

This program will consist of two parts: Part 1 is an educational and fun play. Part 2 is a “hands-on” bike tour around MIT/Cambridge.

  • Part 1. Bike Safety for Dummies 101 - Participants of our bike safety program will attend a fun and informative student-led play on bike safety. They will learn about the real dangers of cycling, rules of the road, and resources for cyclists at MIT.
  • Part 2. Bike Tour around MIT/Cambridge - Participants will jump on Hubway bikes and be guided by MIT Police around the streets of MIT and Cambridge.

Spring Bike Safety Program

Q: Is this program for me?
A: Do you have a bicycle already but still are not clear about the rules of the road? Did you partake in the Fall Bike Safety program previously and now own a bicycle? Did your bike come from the MIT Police bike auction and might now be falling apart? Would you like a helmet and bike lights to go with your registered bike? If yes, then this program is perfect for you!

  • Part 1. Bike Safety for Dummies 202 - Participants of our bike safety program will attend a fun and informative student-led play on bike safety. They will learn about the real dangers of cycling, rules of the road, and resources for cyclists at MIT.
    • Participants will need to bring their own working bicycles and have them registered with MIT PTO. After completing Part I, participants will receive basic bike accessories for a safe ride (e.g., lights, helmets…), have their bike looked over by Cambridge Bicycle for a basic tune-up.
  • Part 2. Bike Tour around MIT/Cambridge - Participants will jump on their own bikes (or Hubway bikes if their bikes are not safe for riding) and be guided by MIT Police around the streets of MIT and Cambridge.
